Contact Me

My name is Hunter Funk and I have always had a passion for video games. I am an intelligent and hardworking person, so life was very stressful with how much work I had and fulfilling the expectations people had around me. However, video games have always served as an escape for me to relax and get away from my responsibilities for a moment. They allow players all over the world to occupy themselves and explore their grandest fantasies in a way other media cannot. I decided that I wanted to become a developer to share with people new experiences and hours of escape that developers before my time gave me. I began Computer Programming in high school as part of a CTE school, West-MEC Central Campus, to take any opportunity I could to get started on my journey. While their I learned copious amounts about professionalism, Web Development, and how coding languages work. I am self-driven and have taken a new passion in learning digital art as well, and I am fascinated by animation and digital illustration. I look forward to hearing what projects and passions you would share with me. I currently attend Arizona State University at its Tempe campus while studying for a BS in Computer Science.

Tanuki Games

For our senior project at West-MEC Central Campus, we were tasked to create a website for a fictional business of our choice. We were also responsible for creating a business proposal as if it were a Shark Tank pitch and how an initial $50,000 investment would be used to keep the company affloat. As someone with a personal interest in game development, I chose to do an Independent Computer Game Developer advertising their new Indie game and asking for support on Kickstarter.

TFT Assist
TFT Assist was a group project where I served as its Team Leader. At the time, we were experimenting with Bootstrap and API's for our first exposure to both. We created a tooltip site for Riot Games' Teamfight Tactics that would use Riot's developer API to fetch user data to display past games, builds, and end results. It was also designed to serve as a tool to analyse the best builds and highest win rates to help players find what was the most optimal choices to win. The site is not operational as intended, and is currently in a state to simply apply methods and knowledge as to how to manipulate api's.
Law and Public Safety RMS
This project involved myself and a small team of 3 other developers to work with the local Law and Public Safety CTE at our school to create an RMS (Report Manager System) application to help file reports as if they were real officers in the police force. We worked with Firebase/Firestore and Material-UI to create and display database information for the user to write, edit, view, and delete different entries. While serving as the Team Leader, I had my hands mostly in front-end development while also helping with databases on the back-end.
